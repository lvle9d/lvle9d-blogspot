Ginza Festival

The Ginza Festival is a wonderful Japanese Festival held annually at the Midwest Buddhist Temple where the members serve food and educate the community about Japanese customs, culture and art. There are plenty events held from drum circles, traditional plays and judo demonstrations held by local dojos. Enjoy authentic Japanese cuisine while you watch the shows or take it around to observe all the art and jewelry vendors from various areas of the States. Take time to view the inside of the beautiful temple and sit in and learn about buddhist culture and customs. Share stories with all people alike whom share the same affinity of Japanese culture. This is one of the many great summer events held in the diverse city of Chicago.

Montrose Beach

There are plenty of scenic views of the city but I am partial to Montrose Beach. Here you’ll find plenty of things to see such as skateboarders tearing up a bowl, legions of dogs wrestling each other in the sand, volleyball tournaments, early morning local fisherman, nature walks for the bird lovers and last but not least an amazing view of the city’s breathtaking landscape. I like going in the morning time for long walks and chilling in the park, its often you see plenty of people trying to do the same catching rays of sunshine and or working their sailboats at the docks. The beach is never short of views and events in the summertime. Here they host several events as well from marathons to music festivals. Needless to say its impossible to bored when you’re visiting one of the lakes most amazing features.
